# AI Model News - February 24, 2026

## Kimi K2.5 Funding Surge: $700M Round Drives Revenue Explosion

Moonshot AI, creator of the Kimi K2.5 large language model, has secured over $700 million in new funding as revenues skyrocket. Sources report that K2.5 has generated more revenue in the past 20 days than the company earned in all of 2025, driven by surge in global paying users and API usage.

The timing coincides with K2.5's reputation as one of the strongest open-weight alternatives to GPT-5 and Gemini for coding tasks, offering multimodal capabilities with vision and agent swarm execution.

## DeepSeek Next-Generation Model Incoming

Chinese AI startup DeepSeek is preparing to release a major new AI model "as soon as next week," according to senior Trump administration officials. The model was reportedly trained using Nvidia's most advanced Blackwell chips, potentially representing a significant advancement in China's AI capabilities.

This follows DeepSeek's pattern of releasing competitive models that challenge Western AI dominance.

## Gemini 3.1 Pro Production Readiness

Google DeepMind's Gemini 3.1 Pro continues gaining adoption in enterprise environments, with reports highlighting its 1M-token context window and 77.1% ARC-AGI-2 performance making it the "most advanced Pro-tier model as of February 2026."

The model's integration into GitHub Copilot for agentic coding workflows is seeing increased developer adoption, particularly for multimodal reasoning tasks across text, images, audio, and video.

## Canva Expands AI Portfolio with Dual Acquisitions

Canva announced the acquisitions of MangoAI and Cavalry, two companies positioned to accelerate Canva's AI and professional design roadmap. The move represents Canva's continued push into AI-powered creative tools, potentially intensifying competition in the AI creative suite space.

Details on the acquisition values and specific technology integrations have not been disclosed, but the timing suggests Canva is responding to the rapid advancement of AI creative platforms.
