# AI Model News - March 11, 2026

## Video Generation: LTX-2.3 Addresses Open-Source Quality Gap

Lightricks has released LTX-2.3, a significant upgrade to their open-source video generation model that directly addresses two of the most common creator complaints: soft detail and poor audio quality. Released under Apache 2.0 license, the model represents a meaningful step forward for local video generation capabilities.

**Key Improvements:**
- New VAE architecture delivering sharper fine details, textures, and facial features
- Substantially improved native audio generation that matches visual timing
- Support for both landscape and portrait 9:16 aspect ratios
- 24 FPS and 48 FPS options for smoother motion
- Last-frame interpolation for seamless transitions between shots
- LoRA fine-tuning support for custom styles and characters

**Technical Significance:**
LTX-2.3 uses a DiT-based (Diffusion Transformer) architecture optimized for practical local execution on consumer hardware. The focus on unified audio-video generation where sound is created alongside visuals represents a more cohesive approach than post-processing audio separately.

**Market Context:**
While closed-source models like Seedance 2.0, Kling 3.0, and Veo 3.1 dominate quality benchmarks, LTX-2.3 provides a compelling open-source alternative for creators who need local execution, commercial use rights, or custom fine-tuning capabilities. The Apache 2.0 license removes barriers that limit many other open models.
